Ancient History Gallery

Step into the ancient world and uncover the stories left behind by early civilizations. From the deserts of Egypt to the valleys of the Indus, these artifacts reveal the knowledge, culture, and daily life of our ancestors.

Golden Mask of Tutankhamun

This iconic funerary mask of the young Pharaoh Tutankhamun, made of gold and semi-precious stones, represents the wealth, artistry, and religious beliefs of Ancient Egypt. It was discovered in the Valley of the Kings in 1922 and remains one of the most famous symbols of ancient Egypt.

The Rosetta Stone

The Rosetta Stone was the key to unlocking Egyptian hieroglyphs. Inscribed with the same decree in three scripts—Greek, Demotic, and hieroglyphic—it allowed scholars to finally decode the ancient writing system, opening a new chapter in understanding Egyptian history and language.

Code of Hammurabi

One of the earliest written legal systems, the Code of Hammurabi was created by the Babylonian king around 1754 BCE. Carved into a tall stone stele, it contains laws covering trade, property, family, and justice—famously known for the principle “an eye for an eye.”

The Acropolis of Athens

The Acropolis, perched high above Athens, was the heart of Ancient Greek culture and democracy. The Parthenon, its most famous structure, stands as a symbol of architectural brilliance, civic pride, and worship of the goddess Athena. It remains a monument to classical Greek civilization.

Mohenjo-daro Citadel

Mohenjo-daro, part of the Indus Valley Civilization (circa 2500 BCE), showcased advanced city planning with grid-pattern streets, drainage systems, and public baths. The Great Bath and the Citadel reflect a society that valued cleanliness, organization, and sophisticated urban life.
