Art Gallery

Discover timeless masterpieces from different cultures and eras. From the Renaissance to modern abstract, this gallery celebrates artistic expression in its many forms.

Mona Lisa – Leonardo da Vinci

One of the most famous paintings in the world, the Mona Lisa is known for her mysterious smile and the use of sfumato technique. Painted in the 16th century, this Renaissance masterpiece continues to intrigue viewers at the Louvre Museum in Paris.

The Starry Night – Vincent van Gogh

This swirling night sky, painted from Van Gogh's asylum room in 1889, expresses deep emotion through bold brush strokes and color. It’s considered one of the most powerful representations of post-impressionist art.

The Scream – Edvard Munch

A symbol of modern existential angst, "The Scream" conveys raw emotion through distorted forms and a turbulent background. Created in 1893, it remains one of the most iconic images in the history of modern art.

Guernica – Pablo Picasso

Painted in 1937, Guernica is a powerful anti-war mural that responds to the bombing of a Basque town. Picasso used a monochrome palette and dramatic figures to express suffering, pain, and chaos caused by war.

The Persistence of Memory – Salvador Dalí

This surrealist masterpiece, created in 1931, features melting clocks in a dreamlike landscape. It reflects Dalí's interest in time, subconscious, and the fluid nature of reality, making it a cornerstone of surrealist art.
